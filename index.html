What I want my work to show

This portfolio is set up to show more than a final screen. I wanted the case study to read like a real build process: what the original experience was struggling with, how the design changed, where testing mattered, and what I personally touched when the team moved from concept to something that actually worked.

Clear problem framing

I try to explain what was not working in plain language first, because a redesign only makes sense if the problem is obvious to someone reading it for the first time.

Process over hype

I would rather show the draft, the broken version, the revised version, and the reason for each change than pretend the first idea was perfect.

Practical front end thinking

The parts I enjoy most are the pieces that make a site feel finished: search behavior, navigation, overlays, spacing, and making sure page elements actually connect and function.

What I learned on this project

  • Search feels simple to users, but there is a lot going on underneath once overlays, interactions, and links all have to behave correctly.
  • Cleaning up navigation can do as much for usability as adding a brand new feature.
  • Hard coded solutions can get something working fast, but they become harder to maintain once the project grows.
  • It is easier to debug layout and interaction issues when the structure is consistent across pages.
